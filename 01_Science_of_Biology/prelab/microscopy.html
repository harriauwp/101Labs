Micro-Module: Designing Experiments

A controlled experiment is one in which only one variable is changed at a time, while all others are kept constant. This allows the researcher to determine if the specific variable being tested is responsible for the observed results.

Key Definitions:

  • Independent Variable (IV): The factor you purposefully change or manipulate (the "cause").
  • Dependent Variable (DV): The factor you measure or observe (the "effect/data").
  • Control Variables: Factors kept exactly the same for all groups to prevent "noise" in the data.
  • Control Group: The baseline group that does not receive the experimental treatment.
