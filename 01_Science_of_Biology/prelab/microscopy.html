Micro-Module: The Compound Microscope

The compound light microscope is a precision instrument that uses two lens systems (ocular and objective) to magnify small specimens. Proper handling and understanding of its optics are essential for success in the lab.

Core Concepts:

  • Total Magnification: Calculated by multiplying the Ocular lens (usually 10X) by the Objective lens (4X, 10X, 40X, or 100X).
  • Inversion: The microscope optics flip the image both vertically and horizontally.
  • Field of View (FOV): The visible area seen through the oculars. As magnification increases, the FOV decreases.
  • Depth of Field: The thickness of the specimen that is in sharp focus.
