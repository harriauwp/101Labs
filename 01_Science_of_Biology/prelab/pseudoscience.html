Micro-Module: Science vs. Pseudoscience

Pseudoscience consists of statements, beliefs, or practices that claim to be both scientific and factual but are incompatible with the scientific method.

Red Flags of Pseudoscience:

  • Anecdotal Evidence: Relying on personal "success stories" rather than controlled data.
  • Lack of Falsifiability: Making claims that cannot be proven wrong by any possible physical evidence.
  • Absence of Peer Review: Pitching ideas directly to the public or media without scientific scrutiny.
  • Static Beliefs: Refusing to change a claim even when contradictory evidence is presented.
