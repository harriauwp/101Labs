Why is the course structured this way?

This lab structure incorporates research-validated learning strategies to maximize long-term retention and mastery.

Spaced Practice

The Strategy: Spacing learning over time rather than "cramming" all at once.

Implementation: Rolling deadlines (48h, 24h, 12h) force you to spread your interaction with the material over several days.

Source: Carpenter et al., 2012; Cepeda et al., 2006

Retrieval Practice

The Strategy: Actively recalling information from memory rather than just re-reading it.

Implementation: Frequent low-stakes quizzing (Micro-Pretests) ensures you are constantly "pulling" information out of your brain.

Source: Roediger & Karpicke, 2006

Interleaving

The Strategy: Mixing different topics together rather than studying them in blocks (AAABBB vs ABCABC).

Implementation: The Post-Lab Mastery section mixes topics to improve your ability to discriminate between concepts and apply the correct solution.

Source: Taylor & Rohrer, 2010

Feedback with Explanations

The Strategy: Immediate correction of errors prevents misconceptions from taking root.

Implementation: Every quiz question provides immediate feedback explaining why an answer is correct or incorrect.

Source: Little & Bjork, 2015
