🍞 Lab 2: Carbohydrates

Exploring the Structure and Function of Life's Energy Molecules

← Back to Labs

📋 Lab Objectives

  1. Understand the chemical composition of living cells, focusing on water and carbohydrates.
  2. Learn the properties of water, including polarity and covalent bonding.
  3. Review basic organic chemistry concepts: carbon compounds, functional groups, and bonding.
  4. Identify and define polymers, monomers, dehydration, and hydrolysis reactions.
  5. Explore the structure and function of carbohydrates: monosaccharides, disaccharides, and polysaccharides.
  6. Understand glucose transport and storage in plants and animals.
  7. Visualize structural isomers and functional groups through molecular models.
  8. Learn the differences between starch, glycogen, and cellulose and their biological roles.
  9. Perform iodine tests to detect starch and understand its distribution in plant cells.
  10. Examine plant cell types (parenchyma, collenchyma, sclerenchyma) and their functions.
  11. Study vascular tissues (xylem and phloem) and their roles in transport.
  12. Analyze the structure and function of a sunflower stem and identify its regions and cell types.
  13. Observe sclereids in pear tissue and understand their role in plant rigidity.

🔑 Key Concepts

Carbohydrate Types

  • Monosaccharides: Simple sugars like glucose and fructose (single units)
  • Disaccharides: Two sugar units linked together (e.g., sucrose, maltose)
  • Polysaccharides: Long chains of sugar units (e.g., starch, glycogen, cellulose)

Important Reactions

  • Dehydration Synthesis: Joining molecules by removing water
  • Hydrolysis: Breaking molecules apart by adding water

Plant Cell Types

  • Parenchyma: Storage and basic metabolic functions
  • Collenchyma: Support in growing regions
  • Sclerenchyma: Rigid support in mature tissues

Vascular Tissues

  • Xylem: Transports water and minerals upward from roots
  • Phloem: Transports sugars and nutrients throughout the plant
