Step 3 — Analyze BIOS101

← Back to Hub

Purpose

This step identifies what BIOS101 actually measures. Difficulty here is not about intelligence — it is about mismatched preparation.

Core Course Themes

  • Scientific Method & Experimental Design
  • Cell Structure & Function
  • Biochemistry Basics
  • Genetics
  • Photosynthesis
  • Mitosis & Meiosis

Skill Emphasis

  • Conceptual understanding of hypothesis testing, variables, replication
  • Metric conversions and microscope size estimation
  • Structure–function relationships
  • Interpretation of diagrams, tables, and experimental data

Vocabulary Load

High-frequency technical terms appear repeatedly and are assumed to be recalled quickly:

  • hypothesis, independent variable, dependent variable
  • chromatin, osmosis, glycosidic linkage
  • control group, experimental group

Identify Likely Failure Points

  • Units & Conversions: nano, micro, milli; exponential notation
  • Variables: independent vs dependent vs control
  • Similar Terms:
    • hypothesis vs theory vs law
    • qualitative vs quantitative
    • prokaryotic vs eukaryotic
    • mitosis vs meiosis
  • Microscopy: magnification vs resolution vs contrast
  • Biochemistry: dehydration vs hydrolysis

What BIOS101 Actually Tests

  • Recall + Application: definitions AND lab-based use
  • Procedural Thinking: logic of experimental design
  • Analytical Skills: graphs, ratios, predictions
  • Data Handling: metric conversions, Punnett squares
  • Integration: structure → function across levels

Memory & Thinking Demands

  • Encoding: organized categories and visual anchors
  • Retrieval: rapid recall under time pressure
  • Higher-Order Thinking: compare, explain, synthesize
