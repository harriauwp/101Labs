Step 4 — Design Your Strategy

← Back to Hub

Purpose

You now know what BIOS101 demands. This step is about matching strategies to those demands. Guessing is replaced with hypothesis-driven design.

Demand → Strategy Mapping

BIOS101 Demand Required Cognitive Action
High vocabulary load Rapid, repeated retrieval of precise definitions
Experimental design logic Active reconstruction of procedures and variable roles
Microscopy & units Frequent low-stakes calculation and estimation practice
Similar biological terms Compare–contrast discrimination
Structure–function integration Explain cause–effect relationships verbally

Strategy Selection

  • Retrieval practice (active recall)
  • Spaced repetition over multiple days
  • Interleaving similar concepts
  • Elaboration through explanation
  • Error analysis and correction

Constraint Check

A strategy is useless if it violates your real constraints. Select your limiting factors.

Hypothesis Formation

Write a testable statement linking strategy to outcome.

Prediction

What do you expect to change if this strategy is applied correctly?

Failure Anticipation

What is most likely to cause this plan to fail?
